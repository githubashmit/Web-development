FAQs of India's Air Quality Index

(Frequently Asked Questions)


Some facts about air pollution level in India and what quality of air you breathe in your region.


1. What is the current Air Quality Index (AQI) in India?

The real-time air quality in India is 92 MODERATE AQI now. This was last updated 1 minute ago .

2. What is the current concentration of PM2.5 in India? Should I be worried?

The current concentration of PM2.5 in India is 57 (µg/m³). The World Health Organisation (WHO) recommends 15 µg/m³ as the threshold concentration of PM2.5 for 24 hrs mean. Currently, the concentration is 2.28 times the recommended limit.

3. What season is the worst air quality in India?

Generally, the air quality at India starts deteriorating in late October. The winters are the worst-hit season in terms of air pollution.

4. When was the best air quality at India in the last 24 hrs?

5. When was the worst air quality at India in the last 24 hrs?

6. What should I take care of before going outdoor when India AQI is very high?

You should wear a good N95 mask when you go outdoor in India until the AQI is improving upto moderate range.

7. How can office going people or anyone avoid air pollution in India?

Office going people should avoid personal vehicles and use public transportations or carpooling.

8. What are the primary causes of outdoor and indoor air pollution in India?

(i) The primary causes of outdoor air pollution are solid, liquid particles called aerosols & gase from vehicles emissions, construction activities, factories, burning stubble & fossil fuels and wildfire, etc.

(ii) Main causes of indoor air pollution are harmful gases from cooking fuels (such as wood, crop wastes, charcoal, coal and dung), damp, mould smoke, chemicals from cleaning materials, etc.

9. How harmful is indoor air pollution in India and why?

Indoor air pollution in India is as dangerous as outdoor pollution, because the air pollutants come inside the houses or buildings through doors, windows and ventilation.

10. What is the solution for indoor air pollution in India?

In India, you must use an air purifier or fresh air machine at home or office indoor and close all the doors, windows and ventilations when the outdoor air quality index (aqi) in India is very high. Proper ventilation is highly recommended only when outdoor air quality is improving and moderate AQI range.
